### I’m Jamie Curle.

I’ve been designing, developing, and deploying web applications (_and all sorts of web “stuff”_) since the late 1990s. I worked in various individual contributor (IC) roles (_design/development_) until 2016, when I started transitioning into engineering management (EM) roles (_senior, lead, head and eventually CTO & CIO_). I did that not out of ambition, but because out of the team at the time, I was best placed to make that move in order to support the people I was leading. I'm a generalist problem solver at heart and so as long as there's a problem, I don't mind what tools (_leadership, design, code_) I use to deliver solutions to problems. However by 2022 I was feeling the itch to start fresh in a more on hands on IC role. I missed the writing of code.

So, in 2022, I started my own company (_Trees & Code Limited_) to return to my “on the tools” (IC) roots. Around the same time, I bought an ancient semi-natural woodland (_ASNW_) and because I had the time (_running your own company has some benefits_) I took the time out to become a qualified arborist and woodland manager (_NPTC and Level 3 Arb_). Arb world stands to gain a lot from recent technology advancements and I'm long-term invested in that outcome. I fell deep into the arb & woodland management rabbit hole and even considered a full career switch, but once again the software engineering itch returned.

Problem is, after three years working alone, I'm also once again feeling that collaborative itch. I miss the interactions with people and the problem solving power a group of people bring. An AI LLM is no replacement for sentient meat in terms of interactions :)

---

### Looking for the right role

I've got a nice balance in my life now. I have the woodland to get me outside and sweating whilst I work with trees and woodland ecology. I have my small home workshop as a creative outlet to tinker with wood and increasingly, 3D prints and hobbyist electronics. But what I'm lacking, and what I'm looking for, is anm IC software engineering role working with people. I don't mind remote or a few days in an office.

So I'm looking for an IC role where I can fit in and be challenged.

Until I find that role, I'll be chugging along in my long-term startup [Trees & Code][2]. It's my moonshot idea to level up arborists all around the world with accessible hardware and software to assist them in managing and caring for tree populations in urban and woodland settings. It's half passion project and half software consulting to bring in an income whilst I find the role I'm looking for.
